# Example: Product Org Vision → Board Pitch Video

## Context
- **Source material**: "Day in the Life of a Customer-Whisperer PM" — a narrative document describing a PM's AI-augmented workday in 2027
- **Audience**: Board of PE/VC investors (Insight Partners & Blackstone)
- **Duration**: 120 seconds
- **Arc**: Transformation Arc (primary) + Bookend sub-pattern (secondary)
- **Design system**: Diligent Design System
- **Key message**: 10X productivity through AI doesn't mean headcount reduction — it means 50% efficiency gained + 5X impact through faster PMF and cycle time compression
- **Protagonist**: The Product Org as collective (not a named individual)

## Decisions Made During Creation

### Source Analysis
The narrative doc contained 12+ episodes across a full day. For 120 seconds (~300 words of VO), only 3–4 could survive.

### Moment Selection
The user selected:
1. **6:45 AM agent cluster overnight monitoring** — AI working while humans sleep
2. **7:30 AM Tokyo customer co-design call** — real-time collaboration with prototype
3. **9:15 AM prototype-first planning meeting** — no slide decks, working code with data
4. **6:00 PM Tokyo follow-up** — same customer, same day, board-ready prototype

The Tokyo morning + evening pairing created a natural **bookend** within Scene 3 — the emotional through-line of the video.

### Arc Application
- **Scene 1 (Problem)**: Generic product org pain — months from insight to feature
- **Scene 2 (Shift)**: One-sentence pivot — "At Diligent, we're building a different kind of product organization"
- **Scene 3 (Workflow)**: Three beats — Morning Tokyo, Prototype-First Org, Evening Tokyo
- **Scene 4 (Results)**: Metric-forward close — 50% efficiency + 5X impact data cards

### Audience Calibration
For sophisticated PE/VC investors:
- Documentary narrator tone, not salesperson
- "Show don't tell" — usage data and customer reactions over adjectives
- Explicitly addressed the HC reduction concern: "Every PM, every designer, every engineer is still here"
- Closed with investment-grade metrics, not a brand tagline
